Created 2026. LOGICOFLEARNING The 'Logic of Learning' is a framework of underlying principles that makes learning coherent, meaningful, and purposeful. It moves beyond asking what works in teaching to asking why it works, examining the reasoning behind instructional decisions across the core components of the educative process and from the science of learning. These include the management of cognitive load, the intentional development of knowledge and skills, the meaningful application of content, and the purposeful use of time as a measure of effective teaching. It helps build accountability for designing learning experiences where clarity of purpose, rigor, and relevance are in deliberate balance, and where students are active participants in making meaning. Ultimately, the logic of learning is made visible through assessment, not merely as measurement, but as the mirror that reflects the outcomes of thoughtful, well-structured teaching, and the genuine understanding it produces.
